  • -- Diabetes mellitis type 2:
  • -- Goal for HbA1c is at or below 6.5 ideally but 7.0 is acceptable for elderly patients or those with risk of hypoglycemia or other confounding factors. Glucometer results targeted to be less than 80 mg/dl before meals and less than 180 mg/dl post prandial. Annual optometry exam, neuropathy and peripheral vascular disease screening, flu shot, and age appropriate vaccines. Idealized therapy for any co-morbid conditions (weight, hypertension, lifestyle, smoking, etc). Assess HgA1c and microalbuminuria every six months.
  • -- Target BMI: Ideally 19 25 but up to 30 acceptable if confounding factors present. Dietary goals geared to help control blood sugar and preferably naturally rich in nutrients and low in fat and calories. Avoid sugars and saturated fats where possible. The DASH diet and Mediterranean Diet are good examples to follow.
  • -- Insulin therapy goals should be adjusted in those patients who can tolerate it to achieve blood glucose levels between 80 and 130 mg/dl before meals. If post prandial checks are performed, levels should be less than 180 mg/dl.
